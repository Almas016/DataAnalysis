1. About the Dataset¶

Kolesa.kz - this is not just an online free classifieds board, but a real marketplace and electronic directory for motorists. The website has advertisements from individuals and companies and hold the most voluminous block of information for those who want to buy / sell any type of vehicles (cars, including new cars, motorcycles, boats and boats), spare parts, special equipment, as well as find / offer automobile services.

The dataset is parsed data from the website which contains all of the ads of light vehicles for Almaty region in June month of 2020. It containt over 19k ads across 10 variables such as car brand, price, year of manufacturing, engine size, color, wheel placement, transmission, type of base, and the link to original ad (might not be actual).

Hypothesis Testing¶

The null hypothesis (H0) and (two-tailed) alternative hypothesis (H1) of the one sample T test can be expressed as:

  • H0: = x ("the sample mean is equal to the proposed population mean")
  • H1: x ("the sample mean is not equal to the proposed population mean")

where is a constant proposed for the population mean and x is the sample mean.

One sample t-test¶

Suppose the mean of engine size of light vehicles of base in Kazakhstan is 2.0 (proposed population mean) We would like to test if our sample (Almaty) dataset is different from population mean.

Research Question:

  • Is there a difference between engine mean in our sample and population mean?

Hypothesis:

  • H0 - There is no difference
  • HA - There is a difference
